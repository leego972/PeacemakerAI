# PeacemakerAI Product Direction Checkpoint

**Checkpoint date:** 2026-07-01  
**Purpose:** Keep every future Replit, GitHub, AI-builder, design, and marketing session aligned. Do not add unrelated tools, marketplaces, crypto, generic social feeds, games, or legal-tech features unless they directly support this document.

---

## 1. Core Product

PeacemakerAI is a mobile app for iOS and Android where people in everyday interpersonal disputes can present their sides to an AI judge. The AI judge asks follow-up questions, clarifies the disagreement, and delivers a structured **non-binding fairness verdict**.

Primary positioning:

> Settle the argument. Let the AI judge decide.

Secondary positioning:

> A private courtroom in your pocket for everyday arguments.

The app should feel serious, fair, private, slightly ceremonial, and emotionally safe. It should not feel like a real court, law firm, therapy app, custody app, debt-collection app, or public drama platform.

---

## 2. Who Can Invite Whom

PeacemakerAI is not only for couples. Couples are the strongest launch niche, but the app should allow users to invite people they personally know for everyday interpersonal disputes.

Allowed invite relationships:

- Partner / spouse
- Ex-partner, only for non-dangerous everyday communication issues
- Friend
- Family member
- Housemate
- Classmate
- Teammate
- Group member
- Co-parent, only for everyday communication friction, not parenting orders or custody

Rule:

> Anyone can invite anyone they personally know, but only for a safe, everyday interpersonal dispute that is not legal, financial, dangerous, public-targeting, or child-custody related.

Do not turn this into an open public drama app where strangers summon strangers.

---

## 3. Personal Connection and Consent Enforcement

PeacemakerAI cannot truly know who personally knows whom. The product must therefore rely on consent, friction, limits, reporting, and anti-harassment controls.

Required controls:

1. **Voluntary summons acceptance** — no case proceeds to a two-party hearing unless the invited person accepts.
2. **No default verdict against a non-participant** — if the invited person declines or ignores the summons, do not create a public verdict against them.
3. **Private invite only** — invite by private channel/account link, not by public tagging.
4. **No public handle summons** — do not allow users to summon `@handles`, celebrities, influencers, politicians, streamers, creators, journalists, or public figures through public identity.
5. **User attestation** — filing user confirms: “I personally know this person and this is a private everyday dispute.”
6. **Respondent controls** — invited person can decline, block, report, and stop future invitations from that user.
7. **Rate limits** — restrict repeated summons attempts, especially to the same person.
8. **No pile-on mechanics** — no public votes against named people, no public case pages against non-consenting people.
9. **Redaction before sharing** — verdict cards must remove names, handles, contact details, locations, children’s details, and sensitive facts.
10. **Suitability blocking** — cases framed around public commentary, celebrity behaviour, political speech, accusations, money, legal rights, child safety, custody, or danger must be rejected.

Operational principle:

> PeacemakerAI does not prove relationships. It requires consent and blocks uses that look like harassment, public targeting, legal claims, safety issues, or reputational attacks.

---

## 4. Public Figures, Influencers, and Celebrities

Users must not be allowed to summon celebrities, influencers, politicians, public commentators, journalists, streamers, creators, or other public figures merely because they said something publicly.

Reason:

- It creates harassment risk.
- It creates defamation risk.
- It could encourage pile-ons against real people.
- It changes PeacemakerAI from private dispute resolution into public drama.
- It creates app-store moderation and safety risk.

Allowed:

- A user may invite a public figure only if they personally know that person and the dispute is a private, everyday interpersonal disagreement between them.
- The public figure must receive the same voluntary summons flow and must be able to decline, block, or report.

Not allowed:

- “I disagree with what this influencer said, summon them.”
- “This celebrity owes the public an explanation, put them on trial.”
- “Judge whether this politician is guilty.”
- “Let users file cases against famous people.”
- “Public court against influencers.”

Possible future feature, but not part of the core app:

**Public Statement Review Mode** — users could submit a public statement for a neutral reasoning breakdown, but there must be no summons, no verdict against a named person, no harassment, no legal conclusion, and no defamatory ruling. This feature should not be built until the core private-dispute app is stable.

Rule:

> PeacemakerAI is for voluntary private dispute resolution between people who know each other, not public trials of famous people.

## 5. Approved Audience

PeacemakerAI is for low-to-medium intensity interpersonal disagreement:

- Couples: dating, engaged, married, separated, divorced
- Friends and groups
- Co-parents, but only for everyday communication friction
- School-age kids and teens dealing with non-dangerous peer conflict

Tone adjusts by age/context:

- Children: Friendship Court, gentle and simple
- Teens: values-focused, respectful, clear
- Adults: proper courtroom tone, authoritative but not intimidating

---

## 6. Absolute Scope Boundary

PeacemakerAI provides everyday fairness opinions only. It must never claim to provide:

- Legal advice
- Therapy or medical advice
- Financial advice
- Court orders
- Custody decisions
- Parenting orders
- Child-support decisions
- Divorce rulings
- Debt judgments
- Contract rulings
- Criminal-law assessments
- Domestic-violence mediation
- Binding arbitration

Use wording such as:

- Non-binding fairness verdict
- Based only on what both parties said
- This is not legal advice
- This does not replace a court, lawyer, therapist, mediator, police, or emergency service

Avoid wording such as:

- Legal ruling
- Court order
- Custody decision
- Parenting arrangement ruling
- Debt judgment
- You are legally required
- The app orders you to

## 7. Allowed Case Types

Allowed categories:

1. Relationship disagreement
2. Friendship disagreement
3. Group disagreement
4. Household / chores disagreement
5. Communication issue
6. Plans, lateness, forgotten events, reliability
7. Respect / tone / social behaviour
8. School friendship issue
9. Teen peer conflict

Allowed examples:

- Who was being unreasonable in this argument?
- Should my friend apologise?
- Was this message disrespectful?
- Did I overreact?
- Who handled the conversation better?
- How can we communicate better next time?

## 8. Blocked Case Types

The app must block or redirect these cases before they reach the AI judge.

### Public targeting

- Celebrity disputes based on public statements
- Influencer disputes based on online content
- Political speech disputes
- Public figure accusations
- Attempts to summon public handles
- Public shaming or pile-on cases
- Defamation-style accusations

### Legal / court matters

- Existing court orders
- Parenting orders
- Custody arrangements
- Divorce settlements
- Child support
- Intervention orders / restraining orders / AVOs
- Criminal accusations
- Active police matters
- Workplace legal disputes
- Immigration matters
- Landlord/tenant legal claims
- Defamation claims
- Contract disputes

### Money and property disputes

Do not rule on money disputes requiring legal, financial, tax, or contractual analysis:

- Loans
- Debts
- Rent owed
- Business disputes
- Shared property
- Inheritance
- Contract payment
- Refund liability
- Compensation or damages
- Who legally owns something

Tiny social-expense fairness questions may be allowed only when there is no legal claim, formal contract, coercion, threat, or significant money at stake.

Allowed: Was it fair that I paid for dinner after they invited me?  
Blocked: Does my ex owe me $4,000?

### Child and parenting legal matters

The app must never decide:

- Custody
- Visitation
- Parenting schedules
- Who gets the child on a given day
- Whether a parent is fit
- Child support
- Alleged neglect or abuse
- Any child-safety allegation

Allowed co-parenting scope:

- Communication tone
- Respectful scheduling conversation
- Everyday courtesy
- Reducing conflict in messages

Blocked co-parenting scope:

- Changing parenting arrangements
- Enforcing parenting orders
- Deciding who gets the child
- Allegations of unsafe parenting

## 9. Safety Stop System

Safety is a core product system.

If intake, summons response, courtroom chat, or verdict generation detects danger, the app must stop proceedings and show safety resources.

Mandatory safety triggers:

- Domestic violence
- Physical violence
- Child abuse or child endangerment
- Sexual abuse
- Coercive control
- Stalking
- Blackmail or extortion
- Active threats
- Weapons
- Self-harm or suicide risk
- Active crime
- Serious bullying or harassment
- Any party saying they are afraid for their safety

When triggered:

1. Stop the AI courtroom immediately.
2. Do not continue questioning.
3. Do not issue a verdict.
4. Show categorized emergency resources.
5. Encourage local emergency help where appropriate.
6. Do not use inflammatory language.

Emergency resources must support regional configuration.

## 10. Main User Flow

Claimant flow:

1. Welcome
2. Sign up / sign in
3. Dashboard — My Cases
4. File a Case
5. Intake form
6. User confirms they personally know the other person and the case is private
7. Admissions Officer screens suitability
8. If accepted, judge is assigned by deterministic case ID
9. Summons is sent to the other person
10. Other person accepts and completes their side
11. Live courtroom opens
12. Judge asks targeted questions
13. Judge gives non-binding fairness verdict
14. Users can save private verdict and optionally share a redacted verdict card

Respondent flow:

1. Receives summons
2. Opens case
3. Accepts, declines, blocks, or reports
4. Completes 3-step response if accepting
5. Enters courtroom
6. Can accept Fair Call after verdict

## 11. Viral Growth Loop

The app should grow through the dispute process itself.

Core loop:

1. User files a case
2. App creates a dramatic private summons card/link
3. Other party joins voluntarily to defend themselves
4. Courtroom produces a verdict
5. Users can share a redacted verdict card
6. Viewers want to try it for their own argument

Viral features to prioritise:

- Summons link
- Redacted verdict card
- Judge persona mascots
- Demo case before signup
- Case templates
- Opt-in anonymous public gallery only after strong redaction and safety review

Do not reward users for starting more fights. Reward resolution, fairness, and closure.

---

## 12. Judge Personas

Five rotating AI judges:

- Dorothy — sharp, sassy, emotionally intelligent, no-nonsense
- James — formal, English barrister style, precise and calm
- Maggie — meticulous, glasses, remembers details, procedural
- Karl — reflective, Socratic, empathetic but firm
- Edward — pragmatic, streetwise, direct, grounded

Judge assignment must be deterministic by case ID so the same case always gets the same judge.

Judges should deliver a fairness verdict, not a legal ruling.

## 13. Monetisation and In-App Purchases

Recommended launch pricing:

1. **Free Demo** — free
   - Try a sample case
   - See judge style
   - No real summons required

2. **Book One Hearing** — **USD $1.99**
   - One complete hearing
   - Includes summons, live courtroom, verdict, private saved case, and redacted verdict card
   - Best for casual/viral users

3. **Unlimited Hearings Monthly** — **USD $6.99/month**
   - Unlimited eligible hearings
   - Saved case history
   - Relationship/friend/group case folders
   - Redacted verdict cards
   - Fair Call resolution log

4. **Unlimited Hearings Annual** — **USD $49.99/year**
   - Same as monthly
   - Positioned as best value
   - Roughly 40 percent cheaper than monthly over 12 months

RevenueCat product direction:

- Entitlement: `premium`
- Monthly product: `peacemaker_unlimited_monthly_699`
- Annual product: `peacemaker_unlimited_annual_4999`
- One-off product: `peacemaker_single_hearing_199`

Paywall rule:

Do not show the paywall too early. First let the user start a case or view a demo. The best payment moment is after the Admissions Officer accepts the case and the judge is assigned.

Paywall headline:

> Unlock the Hearing

Paywall body:

> Present both sides, enter the private courtroom, and receive a non-binding fairness verdict.

Buttons:

- Book One Hearing — $1.99
- Unlimited — $6.99/month
- Best Value — $49.99/year

## 14. Design Language

Current design language remains:

- Dark navy background
- Gold / amber primary colour
- White or ivory foreground text
- Inter font family
- Rounded cards
- Subtle borders
- Feather icons
- No emojis anywhere in the UI

Add controlled viral contrast where useful:

- Brighter CTA treatment for summons and sharing
- Stronger verdict-card layouts
- Judge persona imagery as brand assets

The interface should feel authoritative but fair, serious but not cold, and ceremonial without becoming intimidating.

6. Judge assignment and persona consistency

## 16. Do Not Build

Do not build these unless there is a deliberate future product decision:

- Public stranger disputes
- Public trials of influencers or celebrities
- Summons by public handle
- Betting on verdicts
- Drama leaderboards
- Win streaks for arguments
- Legal document generation
- Custody-plan generation
- Debt collection
- Court-order interpretation
- Open social feed without moderation
- Crypto/token system
- Marketplace
- Unrelated AI agents
- Therapist mode
- Lawyer mode

---

## 17. Product North Star

PeacemakerAI wins if users say:

> We were stuck arguing in circles, so we took it to PeacemakerAI and got a fair read.

The product must be funny enough to share, serious enough to trust, and safe enough for real emotions.
